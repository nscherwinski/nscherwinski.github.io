Introduction to the Book of Revelation

The Book of Revelation was written by the book of John in the John was the most well known prophet in Asia Minor, especially around the area of Ephesus. He was the oldest of the Apostles. John wrote his Gospel in 85 AD, and the Book of Revelations was estimated to be written around the turn of the first century in 95 A.D.

The Book of Revelation, of all of the books in the New Testament, has more Old Testament references, even more than any of the 4 gospels by Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John. The book itself, upon first glance, looks extravagant to event the best of readers. This is mainly due to the heavily employed practice of writing with symbolic language. Symbolic Language allows for the important symbols to be conveyed to the reader in such a way in which the reader is able to interpret it in their own way.
